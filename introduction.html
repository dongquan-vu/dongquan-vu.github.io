Research Engineer at Mathematics and Algorithms for Temporal Data (MATD) Lab of Safran Tech .

My main research area is Artificial Intelligent. My current focus is on Bayesian Learning for prognostics and diagnostics of aerospace engines. Beside this, my research interests also lie at the intersection of Online Learning, Optimization and Game Theory with the scope of applications aiming towards Operational Research, Resource Management and Multi-agent Systems.

Recent News:

  • June 2023: IEEE CAI 2023: "A constrained Langevin-adapted Particle Filter for Aircraft Engines’ Health Monitoring" and "Eliminating External Factors with Variables Standardization for Monitoring Applications" were apccepted to be presented in the IEEE Conference on Artificial Intelligence (IEEE CAI 2023).
  • May 2022: ICML 2022: "Scaling up Universal Methods for Convex Optimization" (written in collaboration with Kimon Antonakopoulos, Panayotis Mertikopoulos, Kfir Y. Levy and Volkan Cevher) was apccepted to be presented in the 39th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2022).
  • April 2022: I recently joined the Mathematics and Algorithms for Temporal Data (MATD) Lab at Safran Tech working as a research engineer POLARIS Team.
  • Sep 2021: Great news, I received the "outstanding reviewer award" from Neurips 2021 (top 8% reviewers).
  • Sep 2021: Our work Fast Routing under Uncertainty: Adaptive Learning in Congestion Games with Exponential Weights (in collaboration with Kimon Antonakopoulos, Panayotis Mertikopoulos) has been accepted to present at the 35th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (Neurips 2021).
  • May 2021: Our work “Colonel Blotto Games with Favoritism: Competitions with Pre-allocations and Asymmetric Effectiveness” has been accepted to the 22nd ACM Conference on Economics and Computation (EC'21).
  • June 2020: I have successfully defended my Ph.D. thesis on “Models and Solutions of Strategic Resource Allocation Problems: Approximate Equilibrium and Online Learning in Blotto Games”. It is a Ph.D. on Game Theory and Online Learning.

About me: I am an research engineer working at the Mathematics and Algorithms for Temporal Data Lab of Safran Tech. Prior to this, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at POLARIS team-LIG under the direction of Dr. Panayotis Mertikopoulos. I finished my PhD. in 2020 from Sorbonne Université under the direction of Dr. Patrick Loiseau, Dr. Alonso Silva and Gérard Burnside, focusing on sequential learning in games and Colonel Blotto game. From January 2017 to December 2019, I worked as a junior research engineer at Nokia Bell Labs France, AAAID Lab. Prior to my Ph.D., I received my M.Sc. of Mathematics and Applications degree from Université Paris Saclay in 2016 and my B.Sc. of Mathematics from HCMC University of Education, Vietnam in 2014. I have received First Prize Winner ROADEF Master Students Prize 2016 and Gaspard Monge Master Scholarship Program 2015.
